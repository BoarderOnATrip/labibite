Full Arc Design v2.0

Diapers to Terraforming

Seven stages of hyperscale parenting — from toilet training to building a civilization in your backyard

◆ The 7-Stage Arc

Each stage transforms what "care" means, what the world looks like, what reading is required, and what Carmen's role is. The Bébête grows, has babies, and eventually the colony fills the backyard. The emotional hook escalates from nurture to pride to wonder.

👶

Stage 1: Le Bébé

Helpless blob in a diaper · Carmen is the parent Level C

The Bébête is tiny, round, can't walk, and needs EVERYTHING. It cries, it poops, it's hungry every 30 seconds. Carmen's job is pure survival parenting. This is the hook — the creature is so helpless and cute that she can't look away.

Care Mechanics
  • Feeding — drag bottle/food to mouth. Bébête makes chomping face.
  • Diaper changes — visible dirty diaper appears, Carmen taps to change. Bébête giggles.
  • Potty timing — Bébête makes a "face" (scrunches up). Carmen taps the toilet icon FAST. Success = confetti + "Bravo!" Fail = diaper needed.
  • Sleeping — rock the Bébête by swaying phone/dragging side to side.
  • Crying — tap to pick up. Bébête calms down in Carmen's "arms."
Reading Content
  • Words: pipi, caca, couche, lait, faim, dodo, pleure, propre, sale
  • Speech: "Faim!" "Dodo!" "Pipi!" (1 word)
  • UI labels: Nourrir, Laver, Dormir, Couche
  • Format: 1 word at a time, always with picture + audio
  • Fun factor: "Caca" is inherently hilarious to a 6-year-old. Lean into this HARD.

🚽 The Potty Training Mini-Game

This is the signature mechanic of Stage 1. The Bébête's face scrunches up and it starts squirming. A speech bubble says "Pipi!" or "Caca!" Carmen has a few seconds to tap the toilet. Success: triumphant fanfare, confetti, Bébête does a happy dance on the toilet. Fail: the diaper appears, dirty. TRACK SUCCESSES. Each success fills a "Potty Progress" meter. When it's full, the Bébête graduates from diapers → Stage 2. This gives Carmen a clear, satisfying goal.

🚶

Stage 2: Le Petit

Walking, talking toddler · No more diapers! Level C–D

The Bébête is bigger, can waddle around, and starts talking in short phrases. The diaper mechanic is GONE (huge relief/satisfaction). Now it can walk to the toilet on its own (with encouragement). New mechanic: the Bébête can follow simple instructions, which means Carmen gives commands by reading them.

Care Mechanics
  • Self-feeding — Bébête tries to feed itself (messily). Carmen picks the food.
  • Bath time — interactive scrubbing (from v3), but now with a rubber duck toy.
  • Walking practice — tap to encourage wobbly steps. Bébête falls, gets up, tries again.
  • First words — Bébête tries to say words. Carmen picks the right picture for what it's trying to say.
  • Simple commands — "Viens ici!" "Assieds-toi!" Carmen reads and taps.
Reading Content
  • Words: viens, assieds, mange, bois, donne, regarde, touche
  • Speech: "Je veux manger." "C'est à moi!" (3–4 words)
  • New mechanic: Bébête tries to say a word, gets it wrong. Carmen picks the right word from 2–3 options.
  • Expeditions unlock — Jardin Magique available for supply runs.
🧒

Stage 3: L'Enfant

Curious kid · Carmen becomes the teacher Level D

The Bébête is a full-sized kid now and is CURIOUS about everything. It asks "Pourquoi?" constantly. Carmen's role shifts from caretaker to TEACHER. She teaches it colors, numbers, animals, body parts — by reading the French words and matching them to pictures. The Bébête "learns" what Carmen teaches it.

Care → Teaching Mechanics
  • Teaching colors — "Montre-moi le rouge." Carmen taps the red item.
  • Teaching animals — Bébête sees a picture: "Qu'est-ce que c'est?" Carmen reads the name.
  • Teaching numbers — "Donne-moi trois pommes." Carmen drags the right count.
  • Dress-up — Bébête wants to pick outfits. Labels are in French.
  • Cooking together — simple 2–3 step recipes. Bébête "helps."
Reading Content
  • Sentences: "Montre-moi le bleu." "Donne-moi deux fraises." (5–7 words)
  • Bébête speech: "Pourquoi le ciel est bleu?" "C'est quoi, un papillon?"
  • Carmen reads TO the Bébête — short stories (3–4 sentences) at bedtime. This inverts the power dynamic: she's the reader, it's the listener.
  • New biome: Village des Marchands (more complex shopping).

💡 The Teaching Inversion

This is the pedagogical masterstroke. Carmen doesn't realize she's practicing reading — she thinks she's TEACHING her Bébête. "Show it the blue one" means SHE reads "bleu" to succeed. "Read it a bedtime story" means SHE reads a paragraph in French. Teaching is the most powerful form of learning, and the game makes her the teacher.

💑

Stage 4: La Famille

The Bébête finds a partner · BABIES arrive Level D–E

The original Bébête is now grown enough to have its own partner (a different-colored Bébête arrives). They have BABIES — 2–3 tiny new Bébêtes. Suddenly Carmen is managing a FAMILY. The original Bébête helps with the babies (it learned from Carmen!), but Carmen still oversees everything. The house gets crowded. She needs to build more space.

New Mechanics
  • Baby management — multiple Bébêtes with individual needs. Simple delegation: tap a baby, pick a care action.
  • The original helps! — it feeds babies, changes diapers (callback to Stage 1!). But Carmen must READ instructions for what each baby needs.
  • Building — first construction: expand the house. Read blueprint instructions.
  • Resource management — more mouths = more food needed = more expeditions.
Reading Content
  • Sentences: "Le petit bébé a faim. Donne-lui du lait." (7–8 words)
  • Blueprints: "D'abord, mets les murs. Ensuite, ajoute le toit." (Sequencing!)
  • Multi-character dialogue: Bébêtes talk to each other. Carmen reads conversations.
  • Emotional complexity: "Le bébé pleure parce qu'il est fatigué." (Causal reading)
🏘️

Stage 5: Le Village

Colony grows · Building a community Level E

The babies grow up. More families form. Now there are 8–12 Bébêtes in the backyard. Carmen's role shifts from parent to MAYOR. She builds houses, a school, a market, a farm. Each building has a French sign. Each Bébête has a job (boulanger, fermier, professeur). The backyard starts transforming.

Village Mechanics
  • Building placement — drag buildings onto the backyard grid. Read building descriptions to know what they do.
  • Job assignment — each Bébête has preferences. Read their profile: "J'aime les fleurs. Je veux être fermier."
  • Village problems — "Il n'y a pas assez de nourriture!" Carmen reads the problem and decides what to build or farm.
  • School building — teaches the next generation. Carmen creates "lessons" by picking words.
  • Market economy — Bébêtes trade with each other. Carmen reads trade offers.
Reading Content
  • Signs: "Boulangerie — Pain frais chaque jour!" (8+ word descriptions)
  • Profiles: "Je m'appelle Fleur. J'ai trois ans. J'aime les papillons et le chocolat."
  • Problems: "Le village n'a pas assez d'eau. Il faut construire un puits." (Problem → solution reading)
  • Dialogue: Multi-sentence NPC conversations.
🔬

Stage 6: La Science

Research & discovery · Understanding the land Level E–F

The village is stable, but the backyard soil is limited. The Bébêtes build a research lab. Carmen discovers that the rest of the backyard is "barren" — rocky, dry, lifeless. The Bébête scientists write research reports (in French!) about soil, water, and plants. Carmen reads the research to unlock terraforming technologies.

Science Mechanics
  • Research lab — Bébête scientists conduct experiments. Carmen reads results.
  • Soil analysis — "La terre est trop sèche. Il faut ajouter de l'eau et du compost."
  • Plant experiments — try growing different plants. Read which conditions they need.
  • Weather observation — "Demain, il va pleuvoir. C'est bon pour le jardin!"
  • Invention — Bébêtes invent tools. Carmen reads blueprints to build them.
Reading Content
  • Research reports: 3–4 sentence paragraphs with scientific vocabulary adapted for kids.
  • Instructions: Complex multi-step blueprints with sequencing words.
  • Cause & effect: "Si tu ajoutes de l'eau, les plantes vont pousser."
  • New vocabulary: terre, eau, soleil, graine, pousser, racine, feuille, compost
🌍

Stage 7: Terraformer

Transform the wasteland · Build a world Level F

The final act. Carmen uses everything she's learned — the research, the resources, the colony — to TERRAFORM the barren sections of the backyard into living, thriving ecosystems. She places soil, channels water, plants seeds, and watches forests and meadows grow. The Bébête colony expands into the new territory. The backyard becomes a lush micro-civilization.

Terraforming Mechanics
  • Terrain painting — drag to place soil, water, grass on the barren grid.
  • Ecosystem balance — read indicators: "Les plantes ont besoin de plus d'eau."
  • Colony expansion — Bébêtes move into new zones. Build new buildings.
  • Seasons — the terraform reacts to seasons. Carmen reads seasonal reports.
  • The Grand Finale — the entire backyard is green, the colony is thriving, Bébêtes celebrate. The original Bébête — now an elder — thanks Carmen in a full paragraph of French.
Reading Content
  • Complex instructions: "D'abord, vérifie que la terre est prête. Ensuite, plante les graines. Puis, ajoute de l'eau chaque jour. Finalement, attends que les plantes poussent."
  • Full paragraphs: Ecosystem reports, colony newsletters, elder speeches.
  • Inference required: "Les fleurs ne poussent pas. Qu'est-ce qui manque?" (Carmen figures out it needs more water)
  • Emotional payoff: The original Bébête's final speech — a full paragraph thanking Carmen for everything, from diapers to terraforming.

🎬 The Final Speech

When the terraforming is complete, the original Bébête — now old and wise — gathers the entire colony. It delivers a speech in French that references EVERY stage Carmen went through: "Tu m'as changé les couches quand j'étais petit. Tu m'as appris les couleurs. Tu m'as aidé avec mes bébés. Tu as construit notre village. Tu as transformé notre monde. Merci, mon amie. Merci pour tout." Carmen has to READ this entire paragraph — the final reading challenge — and it will make her cry because she remembers every moment. This is the emotional payoff of the entire game.

◆ Reading Progression Map

Stage 1–2: Survival Words C

1-word commands and labels. "Pipi!" "Faim!" "Couche!" Picture + audio on everything. Carmen reads to survive (keep the Bébête alive).

Stage 2–3: Simple Phrases C–D

3–5 word phrases. "Je veux manger." "Donne-moi le bleu." Carmen reads to teach (show the Bébête what things are called).

Stage 3–4: Teaching Sentences D

5–8 word sentences. "Montre-moi trois pommes rouges." "Le bébé pleure parce qu'il a faim." Carmen reads to nurture (manage a family).

Stage 4–5: Multi-Sentence Reading D–E

Multiple sentences in sequence. Building blueprints, dialogue, problem descriptions. Carmen reads to build (construct a village).

Stage 5–6: Paragraph Reading E

3–4 sentence paragraphs. Village signs, character profiles, research reports. Carmen reads to govern (make decisions for the colony).

Stage 6–7: Complex Paragraphs E–F

Full paragraphs with cause-effect, conditionals, sequencing. Research reports, ecosystem data. Carmen reads to discover (understand science).

Stage 7: Full Text F

Multi-paragraph text. The elder Bébête's final speech. Terraforming manuals. Colony newsletters. Carmen reads because she CAN — she's at Level F.

◆ The Potty Training Deep-Dive

This deserves its own section because it's the signature mechanic of the early game and the thing Carmen will tell her friends about.

How It Works

The Bébête has a hidden "potty timer" — every 30–60 seconds, it needs to go. The TELLS are visual:

Visual Tells (Escalating)
  • Level 1: Bébête starts squirming. Subtle wiggle animation.
  • Level 2: Face scrunches up. Cheeks go red. Legs cross.
  • Level 3: Speech bubble: "Pipi!" or "Caca!" (reading required!)
  • Level 4: Bébête is HOPPING. Urgent. This is the last chance.
  • Too late: Sad face. Diaper appears. Bébête looks embarrassed (not ashamed — it's funny, not punishing).
Success Sequence
  • Carmen taps toilet icon (or drags Bébête to toilet)
  • Bébête waddles to toilet with a relieved face
  • Sits on toilet. Funny "plop" sound.
  • TRIUMPHANT FANFARE. Confetti. Bébête claps.
  • Speech bubble: "Bravo!" "Je suis grand!" "Pas de couche!"
  • Potty Progress meter fills up a notch.

🏆 The Diaper Graduation Ceremony

When the Potty Progress meter is FULL (maybe 15–20 successes), there's a celebration cutscene: the Bébête takes off its diaper, throws it in the air, does a victory dance. Confetti EVERYWHERE. The diaper flies offscreen and never comes back. Carmen gets a "Pas de couche!" achievement badge. The Bébête is visibly bigger now — it evolved to Stage 2. This is the most satisfying moment in the early game.

◆ Why This Arc Works Pedagogically

Emotional Stakes Scale With Reading
  • Stage 1: "I need to read 'pipi' or my Bébête will poop itself" (hilarious urgency)
  • Stage 3: "I need to read 'bleu' to teach my Bébête colors" (nurture motivation)
  • Stage 5: "I need to read this sign to build a bakery" (builder pride)
  • Stage 7: "I need to read this report to terraform the land" (world-shaper ambition)
Carmen's Role Evolves
  • Stages 1–2: Parent (keeping it alive)
  • Stage 3: Teacher (teaching it words)
  • Stage 4: Family manager (handling multiple creatures)
  • Stage 5: Mayor (governing a community)
  • Stage 6: Scientist (understanding the world)
  • Stage 7: Terraformer (reshaping the world)

🎯 The Escalire Mapping

Stages 1–2 cover Escalire Level C (where Carmen is now). Stages 3–4 bring her through Level D. Stages 5–6 tackle Level E. Stage 7 is Level F — the target. But she never sees any of that. She just sees her Bébête colony growing. By the time she's reading full paragraphs to terraform land, she's at Level F without ever having been told she was "practicing reading."

◆ Build Priorities

We don't need all 7 stages on day one. We build Stage 1 first, make it incredible, and layer stages on top.

Sprint 1: Stage 1 — Le Bébé (the potty game)

Canvas Bébête with diaper, feeding, potty timing mini-game, sleeping, crying. The potty progress meter. Diaper graduation cutscene. This alone is a complete, playable, hilarious game.

Sprint 2: Stage 2 — Le Petit + Expeditions

Post-diaper gameplay: walking, bath scrubbing, first words, simple commands. The Jardin Magique expedition for supply runs. Teaching mini-game.

Sprint 3: Stage 3 — L'Enfant (teaching mode)

Color/number/animal teaching games. Bedtime story reading. Dress-up. Village marketplace expedition.

Sprint 4+: Stages 4–7 (colony growth)

Baby arrival, family management, village building, research, terraforming. Each sprint adds a stage.
