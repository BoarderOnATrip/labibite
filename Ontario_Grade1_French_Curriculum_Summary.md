# Ontario Grade 1 (1re annee) French Curriculum -- Learning Expectations Summary
## Extracted from Ontario 2023 French Curriculum Documents
### For: Petit Monde French Game (6-year-old, Grade 1 French Immersion)

---

## 1. PHONICS AND DECODING (Phonetique / Decodage)

### 1A. Phonemic Awareness (Conscience phonemique) -- B2.1
**Grade 1 expectation:** Isolate, segment, and fuse phonemes in spoken words, starting with simple 2-phoneme structures and progressing to more complex structures with more phonemes.

**Segmentation (for writing):**
- Segment words with increasingly complex syllable structures
- Simple syllable structures: CV, CVC
- More complex: CCV, CCVC, CVCC, CVC/CVC, CVC/CV
- Examples:
  - V/CV: ami = /a_m_i/
  - V/CVC: ecole = /e_k_o_l/
  - CCV: trou = /t_r_u/
  - CCVC: flamme = /f_l_a_m/, truc = /t_r_y_k/
  - CVCC: parc = /p_a_r_k/, mars = /m_a_r_s/
  - CVC/CVC: partir = /p_a_r/t_i_r/
  - CVC/CV: jardin = /zh_a_r/d_en/

**Fusion (for reading):**
- Fuse phonemes to form words, starting with 2 phonemes progressing to 5
- Varying syllable structures
- Examples: ami, ecole, trou, flamme, truc, parc, mars, partir, jardin

### 1B. Letter Knowledge (Connaissance des lettres) -- B2.2
**Grade 1 expectation:** Name and form all uppercase and lowercase letters in any order with automaticity; demonstrate knowledge of alphabetical order.

**Specific letter-sound correspondences to master:**
- Vowels: a=/a/, e=/schwa/ or /eh/, i=/i/, o=/o/ or /oh/, u=/y/, y=/i/
- Consonants with single sounds: l, t, d, j, r, n, m, b, v, f, p, k, z
- Consonants with multiple sounds:
  - c = /k/ (before a,o,u) or /s/ (before e,i,y)
  - g = /g/ (before a,o,u) or /zh/ (before e,i,y)
  - s = /s/ (initial) or /z/ (between vowels)
  - x = /ks/ (taxi), /gz/ (examen)
- Silent h (but needed for digraph ch)
- Accented vowels: e=/e/, e-grave and e-circumflex = /eh/
- w = /w/ (wapiti)
- q = /k/ (cinq)

### 1C. Grapheme-Phoneme Correspondences (CGP) -- B2.3
**Grade 1 expectation:** Recognize, read, and spell grapheme-phoneme correspondences for the most frequent consonants and vowels with increasing automaticity.

**Simple CGP (introduction in Grade 1):**
- Use simple, consistent graphemes to read words presented in isolation or in decodable texts
- Choose words easily decoded: simple graphemes, consistent correspondences
- Varied syllable structures: CV, VC, CVC, CCVC, CVCC

**Complex CGP (introduction for 1st year):**
- au, eau = /o/
- ou = /u/
- eu = /oe/
- ai, ei = /eh/
- er = /e/
- oi = /wa/
- ch = /sh/
- qu = /k/
- gu = /g/ (before e, i, y)
- gn = /ny/
- Nasal vowels: an/en = /an/, in = /in/, on = /on/, un = /un/, ain/ein = /in/
- High-frequency patterns: es/et = /eh/, ez = /e/, ill/ille = /ij/
- Semi-consonant combinations: oin = /win/, ion = /yon/, ien = /yin/, ail/aille = /aj/

**Contextual CGP (introduction in Grade 1):**
- g before e,i,y = /zh/ (girafe)
- c before e,i,y = /s/ (citron)
- s between 2 vowels = /z/ (maison)
- ss = /s/ (tasse)
- t before i + vowel = /s/ (recreation)
- Silent e at end of word causes preceding consonant to be pronounced
- Most final consonants are silent (petit, loup)
- Nasal vowels use m before m, p, b (ambulance, important)

### 1D. Word-Level Reading and Spelling -- B2.4
**Grade 1 expectation:** Apply grapheme-phoneme knowledge and fuse phonemes to read words; segment phonemes to spell phonetically regular words, in isolation and in varied text contexts.

**Word structures to read in Grade 1:**
- 1 syllable: CV (bu, ma), VC (il), CVC (lac, fil, balle), CCV (trou, bleu), CCVC (plate, branche, fraise), CVCC (porte)
- 2 syllables: CV/CV (bijou), CV/CVC (tomate), V/CVC (ecole), CCV/CV (drapeau)
- 3+ syllables: CV/CV/CV (magasin), VC/CV/CV/CVC (ordinateur), V/CV/CVC (autobus)

### 1E. Orthographic Regularities -- B2.5
**Grade 1 expectation:** Apply orthographic knowledge, including positional rules, to make spelling choices or pronounce words accurately.

- Nasal vowels before m, b, p use m (bambou, emmener, timbre, ombre)
- c/g soft before e, i, y vs. hard before a, o, u
- gu before e, i, y = /g/ (guenon, guimauve)
- cedilla: c before a, o, u = /s/ (ca, garcon, recu)
- Words ending in consonant sound usually have silent e (banane, tube, tulipe)

### 1F. Morphological Knowledge -- B2.6
**Grade 1 expectation:** Develop and apply knowledge of word meanings and word components (bases, prefixes, suffixes) to read and spell words.

- Recognize and use common prefixes orally: re- (refaire), de- (defaire), in-
- Recognize common suffixes orally: -ette (fillette), -iste (fleuriste)
- Associate words from the same morphological family (fleur, fleuriste, fleurir)
- Find the odd word out from a list of morphologically related words

## 2. VOCABULARY (Vocabulaire) -- B2.7

### Grade 1 expectation:
Demonstrate understanding of the meaning of common words, acquire and use explicitly taught vocabulary in various contexts including other subjects, and develop and apply morphological knowledge to analyze and understand new words in context.

### Key vocabulary strategies:
- Sort, categorize, and classify words by semantic characteristics
- Associate new words with previously known words based on meaning
- Recognize polysemous words (multiple meanings -- e.g., "baguette": bread vs. magic wand)
- Use Level 2 vocabulary words (academic words explicitly taught)
- Decode and understand unknown words from their graphemes and morphological analysis

### Vocabulary categories for Grade 1 (from Lexique section):
**Word families (familles de mots) -- Sensibilization (S) in Grade 1:**
- Same morphological family: saut, sauter, sursauter
- Same semantic family: introduced in Grade 2

**Champs lexicaux (Lexical fields) -- Sensibilization (S) in Grade 1:**
- Choose simple, familiar themes: l'ecole (school), les saisons (seasons)

**Synonyms and Antonyms -- not systematically studied until Grade 2-3:**
- Introduced through reading context

### Recommended thematic vocabulary for Grade 1 game:
Based on the curriculum's emphasis on "simple, familiar themes," target:
1. **L'ecole** -- classe, crayon, livre, cahier, tableau, pupitre, sac, gomme, regle, ciseaux
2. **Les saisons** -- printemps, ete, automne, hiver, neige, soleil, pluie, vent, feuille, fleur
3. **La famille** -- maman, papa, frere, soeur, grand-mere, grand-pere, bebe, famille
4. **Les animaux** -- chat, chien, oiseau, poisson, lapin, cheval, vache, cochon, mouton, poule
5. **Le corps** -- tete, bras, main, pied, jambe, nez, bouche, oeil/yeux, oreille, doigt
6. **La nourriture** -- pomme, banane, lait, pain, fromage, eau, gateau, soupe, salade, jus
7. **Les vetements** -- manteau, pantalon, robe, chapeau, chaussure, chandail, tuque, mitaine
8. **Les couleurs** -- rouge, bleu, jaune, vert, orange, violet, blanc, noir, rose, brun
9. **Les nombres** -- un through vingt (and beyond)
10. **Les jours/mois** -- lundi-dimanche, janvier-decembre

## 3. READING COMPREHENSION (Comprehension en lecture)

### 3A. Using Foundational Knowledge -- C1.1
**Grade 1:** Read and demonstrate comprehension of short, simple texts using knowledge of words, grammar, linking words, sentence structure, and prior knowledge.

### 3B. Text Forms and Genres -- C1.2
**Grade 1:** Recognize simple forms of narrative, descriptive, incitative, and poetic/playful discourse, and text genres associated with them.

### 3C. Text Organizers and Features -- C1.3
**Grade 1:** Recognize text organizers (sequential structure, chronological order) and text features (illustrations, symbols, titles) and explain how they help readers understand meaning.

### 3D. Visual Elements -- C1.4
**Grade 1:** Demonstrate understanding of the relationship between text and its simple visual elements (illustrations, graphics, images).

### 3E. Stylistic Elements -- C1.5
**Grade 1:** Recognize simple stylistic elements (voice, word choice, lexical patterns, sentence structure) and describe their contribution to meaning.

### 3F. Point of View -- C1.6
**Grade 1:** Identify the narrator's point of view and suggest another point of view for the story.

## 4. COMPREHENSION STRATEGIES (Strategies de comprehension) -- C2

### 4A. Pre-reading: Activate Prior Knowledge -- C2.1
**Grade 1:** Use prior knowledge, including personal experiences and text knowledge, to understand the subject of new texts.

### 4B. Pre-reading: Determine Purpose -- C2.2
**Grade 1:** Determine purposes for interacting with texts, including reading for pleasure or searching for information.

### 4C. Verify Comprehension: Predictions -- C2.3
**Grade 1:** Make predictions based on prior knowledge, text features, and evidence from the text.

### 4D. Monitor Comprehension -- C2.4
**Grade 1:** Use strategies including rereading, visualization, and questioning to verify comprehension of simple texts.

### 4E. Make Connections -- C2.5
**Grade 1:** Make connections between ideas in simple texts, personal knowledge and experiences, ideas in other familiar texts, and the world.

### 4F. Identify Key Information -- C2.6
**Grade 1:** Identify important information in a simple text, including the main idea.

### 4G. Reflect on Learning -- C2.7
**Grade 1:** Determine strategies that helped comprehension, including activating prior knowledge and visualization.

---

## 5. CRITICAL THINKING IN LITERACY (Pensee critique) -- C3

### 5A. Literary Devices -- C3.1
**Grade 1:** Recognize simple literary devices (rhyme, alliteration, onomatopoeia) and describe their contribution to meaning.

### 5B. Inferences -- C3.2
**Grade 1:** Make simple inferences from explicit and implicit information to understand simple texts.

### 5C. Text Analysis -- C3.3
**Grade 1:** Analyze simple texts (descriptive, narrative, incitative, poetic/playful) by identifying important information, events, and sequential structure.

## 6. ORAL COMMUNICATION (Communication orale) -- B1

### 6A. Listening Skills -- B1.1
**Grade 1:** Use various effective listening skills, including listening attentively and asking relevant questions in formal and informal contexts.

### 6B. Listening Strategies -- B1.2
**Grade 1:** Identify and use strategies before, during, and after listening to understand information communicated orally and non-verbally.

### 6C. Communication Purpose -- B1.3
**Grade 1:** Determine the purpose and audience for oral communication and use appropriate strategies (taking turns, understanding when to speak) to communicate clearly.

### 6D. Non-verbal Strategies -- B1.4
**Grade 1:** Determine and use oral and non-verbal communication strategies (expression, gestures, body language) to interpret or contribute to meaning.

### 6E. Word Choice in Speech -- B1.5
**Grade 1:** Choose and use appropriate words, including recently learned words, grammar, and coherent expressions and sentences to express ideas orally.

## 7. GRAMMAR AND SENTENCE STRUCTURE (Grammaire) -- B3

### 7A. Sentence Types -- B3.1
**Grade 1:** Recognize and construct various types and forms of simple sentences, including declarative sentences in positive and negative forms.
- Declarative: "Noe mange une pomme." (Systematic study - E)
- Interrogative: "Est-ce que Noe mange une pomme?" (Sensibilization - S)
- Exclamative: "Quelle belle journee!" (Sensibilization - S)
- Imperative: introduced in Grade 2
- Positive/Negative forms: "Elle danse" / "Elle ne danse pas" (Sensibilization - S)

### 7B. Sentence Constituents -- B3.2
**Grade 1 (Sensibilization level):**
- Sentence = subject + predicate (+ optional complement)
- Subject identification: "De qui parle-t-on?"
- Predicate identification: "Qu'est-ce qu'on dit du sujet?"
- Noun: proper/common, gender, number
- Determiners: le/la/les, un/une/des (systematic study - E)
- Pronouns: je, tu, il/elle/on, nous, vous, ils/elles (sensibilization - S)
- Prepositions: a, chez, dans, sur (for place), avec (for means) (systematic - E)
- Conjunctions: et, ou, car, parce que (sensibilization - S)
- Adjectives: awareness level only (S)
- Verbs: awareness of tense changes (S)

### 7C. Punctuation and Capitals -- B3.3
**Grade 1:**
- Capital at start of sentence (mastery - A)
- Capital for proper names (systematic - E)
- Period at end of sentence (systematic - E)
- Comma in lists (sensibilization - S)
- Apostrophe for elision (sensibilization - S)

### 7D. Verb Conjugation -- Grade 1 (all Sensibilization - S)
- Infinitive as dictionary form (manger, finir, prendre, faire)
- Present tense of etre, avoir, aller, faire, dire, aimer, finir, voir, savoir, vouloir, venir, prendre, devoir
- Regular -er verb conjugation pattern (j'aime, tu aimes, il/elle aime, nous aimons, vous aimez, ils/elles aiment)
- Near future (futur proche): "Je vais inventer..."
- Imperfect (imparfait): awareness level

### 7E. Gender and Number -- Grade 1 (Sensibilization - S)
- Gender of animate nouns (tante = f, coq = m)
- Gender by word endings (-age = m, -ette = f, etc.)
- Plural: general rule of adding -s
- Determiner agreement in gender/number

## 8. FLUENCY (Fluidite en lecture) -- B2.8

### Grade 1 expectations:
- Read with fluency simple words, short sentences, and paragraphs in various texts
- Read with precision and appropriate rhythm to support comprehension
- Read aloud with expression
- Read decodable texts aloud with automaticity, precision, and relatively natural rhythm
- Pause at end of sentences respecting punctuation
- Read with appropriate intonation matching sentence meaning
- Use teacher feedback to maintain comprehension during reading

---

## 9. PROGRESSION MARKERS (S / E / A System)

The Ontario curriculum uses a 3-level progression:
- **S (Sensibilisation):** Awareness/exploration -- occasional, emerging learning; partial results or success with support
- **E (Etude systematique):** Systematic study -- regular, planned instruction; student observes, identifies, formulates and verifies rules
- **A (Approfondissement):** Deepening/mastery -- consolidation through new situations; continuous, permanent use

### What Grade 1 students should MASTER (E or A level):
- All letter names and sounds (E)
- Simple grapheme-phoneme correspondences (E)
- Capital at sentence start (A)
- Capital for proper nouns (E)
- Period at end of sentence (E)
- Definite/indefinite determiners (E)
- Simple prepositions of place (E)
- Declarative sentences (E)
- Basic sentence structure: subject + predicate (S progressing to E)

### What Grade 1 students are being INTRODUCED TO (S level):
- Complex grapheme-phoneme correspondences
- Interrogative/exclamative sentences
- Negative form
- Adjective agreement
- Verb conjugation (present tense)
- Comma usage
- Pronoun usage
- Word families
- Synonyms/antonyms awareness

## 10. GAME DESIGN IMPLICATIONS

Based on this curriculum analysis, the Petit Monde French Game for Grade 1 should prioritize:

### Core Phonics Activities:
1. Letter-sound matching (all 26 letters + accented vowels)
2. Syllable blending (CV, CVC, CCV patterns)
3. Simple word decoding with decodable text
4. Complex grapheme recognition (ou, au, eau, ch, oi, on, an, in)

### Core Vocabulary Activities:
1. Thematic word learning (school, seasons, family, animals, body, food, clothing, colors)
2. Word sorting by category
3. Word family recognition (base word + variations)
4. Sight word / high-frequency word recognition

### Core Reading Activities:
1. Decodable sentence reading
2. Picture-text matching
3. Sequential ordering of simple stories
4. Main idea identification
5. Prediction activities

### Core Grammar Activities:
1. Gender matching (le/la, un/une)
2. Simple sentence construction (subject + verb + complement)
3. Capital letter and period placement
4. Singular/plural noun recognition

### Sentence Types to Include:
1. Simple declarative sentences (primary focus)
2. Simple questions (awareness)
3. Exclamations (awareness)
4. Positive and negative forms (awareness)
